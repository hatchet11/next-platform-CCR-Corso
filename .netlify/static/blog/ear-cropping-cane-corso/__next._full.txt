10:T1993,<h2>Is Ear Cropping Required?</h2>
<p>No. CCR Kennels does not automatically crop ears. Ear cropping is entirely elective — it is the personal choice of the purchaser, not a standard part of our breeding program.</p>
<p>That said, we do offer ear cropping as an available service for buyers who want it, and we have a trusted veterinary partner who performs the procedure with laser technology. If you are interested, there is a specific window and process you must follow.</p>
<h2>The Timeline: Why It Has to Happen Early</h2>
<p>Ear cropping must be coordinated and fully paid for <strong>before your puppy reaches 7 weeks of age</strong>. The ideal window is between <strong>4 and 6 weeks old</strong>. After 7 weeks, the cartilage in the ear begins to set and the healing process becomes significantly more difficult — most vets will not perform the procedure past this point.</p>
<p>This means the conversation needs to happen early. If you are reserving a puppy and you want ears cropped, <strong>bring it up at the time of reservation</strong> — do not wait until the week before go-home day.</p>
<h2>Where the Procedure Is Done</h2>
<p>We take puppies to our trusted community veterinary partner:</p>
<p><strong>Quad Counties Veterinary Clinic</strong>
<a href="https://www.quadcovet.com/">quadcovet.com</a></p>
<p>This clinic performs ear cropping using <strong>laser technology</strong>, which offers cleaner cuts, reduced bleeding, and generally faster healing compared to traditional surgical methods. They are experienced with the procedure and familiar with working with large breed puppies.</p>
<h2>Crop Styles: What to Request</h2>
<p>The two most common requests for Cane Corsos are:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Short crop</strong> — a conservative cut that leaves more ear leather. This style has the highest success rate for standing properly as the puppy heals and the cartilage strengthens.</li>
<li><strong>Show crop</strong> — a slightly longer crop commonly seen in the conformation ring. This style requires more diligent aftercare and posting to achieve an upright stand.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Our recommendation is the short crop.</strong> It heals more reliably, stands more consistently, and is well-suited to the Cane Corso's head structure. Unless you are specifically planning to compete in AKC conformation where a show crop may be preferred, the short crop is the practical choice for most buyers.</p>
<p>That said, the final decision is yours. Just know that longer crops require more aftercare commitment and there is always a possibility the ears do not stand perfectly — that is true of any crop style and is part of the risk inherent in an elective cosmetic procedure.</p>
<h2>Cost</h2>
<p>The cost of ear cropping is <strong>set entirely by Quad Counties Veterinary Clinic</strong> and is not controlled or marked up by CCR Kennels.</p>
<p>Current pricing typically ranges from <strong>$250 to $350</strong>, with the final cost depending on the weight of the puppy at the time of the procedure. Heavier puppies require more anesthesia and time, which affects the price.</p>
<p>You will pay the veterinary clinic directly or as arranged through the coordination process. Prices are subject to change — confirm current pricing with the vet when you coordinate.</p>
<h2>What CCR Kennels Handles</h2>
<p>When you request ear cropping through us, here is what we take care of:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Scheduling and coordination</strong> with Quad Counties Veterinary Clinic on your behalf</li>
<li><strong>Transportation</strong> of your puppy to and from the vet for the cropping appointment</li>
<li><strong>Before and after care</strong> of the puppy while it is still in our care</li>
<li><strong>Follow-up appointments</strong> as needed during the healing period prior to go-home day</li>
</ul>
<p>We are invested in this process going smoothly because these are our puppies until go-home day. We will not hand off coordination carelessly.</p>
<h2>What Is Required Before We Can Proceed</h2>
<p>Before any ear cropping can be scheduled, <strong>two things must be completed</strong>:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>All deposits must be collected in full</strong> — your reservation deposit must be paid and cleared.</li>
<li><strong>The deposit contract must be signed</strong> — your signed agreement must be on file with us.</li>
</ol>
<p>We will not initiate ear cropping coordination without both of these in place. This protects everyone involved and ensures we are not making irreversible arrangements for a placement that is not yet confirmed.</p>
<h2>Liability and Risk: What You Need to Understand</h2>
<p>Ear cropping is an elective cosmetic procedure. As with any surgical procedure performed under anesthesia, there are risks — including but not limited to anesthesia complications, infection, improper healing, and the ears not standing as desired.</p>
<p><strong>All financial responsibility, medical outcomes, and results — including dissatisfaction with the appearance of the crop — are the sole responsibility of the purchaser.</strong></p>
<p>CCR Kennels is not responsible for:</p>
<ul>
<li>The outcome of the procedure</li>
<li>How the ears heal or stand</li>
<li>Any medical complications before, during, or after surgery</li>
<li>The veterinary clinic's pricing, availability, or scheduling</li>
<li>Results that do not meet the buyer's aesthetic expectations</li>
</ul>
<p>By requesting ear cropping, you are making an informed elective choice. We facilitate the process as a service to our buyers, but the decision, the cost, and the outcome belong to you.</p>
<h2>How to Request Ear Cropping</h2>
<p>If you are interested in ear cropping for your CCR Kennels puppy:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Let us know at the time of reservation</strong> — the earlier the better. Do not wait.</li>
<li><strong>Ensure your deposit is paid and your contract is signed</strong> before requesting coordination.</li>
<li><strong>Discuss crop style preference</strong> with us so we can communicate it to the vet.</li>
</ol>
